St. Patrick’s Day Team Challenge A → C

6) Cultural Snap

Short reading + comprehension + light opinion.

← Home / QR Grid

How to play

  • Choose a level and read the short text.
  • Answer the questions (short answers are fine).
  • Show your answers to the teacher.
Read:
St. Patrick’s Day is celebrated on March 17. It began in Ireland many years ago. Today people in many countries celebrate the holiday. Many people wear green clothes and join parades. Some cities organize large public events.
  1. When is St. Patrick’s Day?
  2. Where did it begin?
  3. What color do many people wear?
  4. What do cities organize?

Teacher check: 4 correct answers = 1 point.

Read:
St. Patrick’s Day started as a religious holiday in Ireland. Over time, it became a celebration of Irish culture. Irish immigrants brought the tradition to countries like the United States and Canada. In some cities, rivers are dyed green, and large parades are organized. Today the holiday is both cultural and commercial, with businesses selling themed food and clothing.
  1. What was the holiday originally?
  2. How did it spread to other countries?
  3. Name one public event mentioned in the text.
  4. What does “commercial” mean here?

Teacher check: 4 correct answers = 2 points.

Read:
Although St. Patrick’s Day began as a religious celebration, it has changed over time. Irish immigrants helped transform it into a public festival that celebrates Irish heritage and identity. In many countries, the holiday now includes concerts, parades, and large public gatherings. Some critics argue that the holiday has become too commercial, focusing more on parties than cultural meaning. However, others believe it remains an important opportunity to celebrate history and community.
  1. How has the holiday changed?
  2. Why did immigrants play an important role?
  3. What criticism is mentioned?
  4. In your opinion (1–2 sentences): Is it OK for holidays to change over time? Why?

Teacher check: comprehension correct + 1–2 sentence opinion = 3 points.

Teacher scoring: Level A = 1 point, Level B = 2 points, Level C = 3 points. Optional: +1 collaboration token (cap per team or class).
